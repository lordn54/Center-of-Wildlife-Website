Mission

The International Center for Aviation and Wildlife Mitigation (ICAWRM) engages the resources of Embry-Riddle Aeronautical University-Prescott with other institutions including the Federal Aviation Administration, United State Department of Agriculture-Wildlife Services, Department of Defense-Air Force and Navy, Bird Strike International committee, and other concerned entities for the purposes of
  • Collecting, maintaining, and disseminating relevant data and research
  • Developing training programs, management policies, and operational plans
  • Dealing with aviation and wildlife risk mitigation
  • Bridging the gap between the scientific community and stakeholders, including the general public, through effective communication, i.e. (press releases, public forums, how-to handbooks, web site, newsletters )
The ICAWRM will fulfill this mission by stimulating and supporting research and education activities among the various academic departments and research organizations of the university through contracts and grants.

Background

Over the past 20 years University Centers have emerged as clearinghouses with the purpose of collecting, maintaining, analyzing, and disseminating scientific information. In a world of rapid scientific and technological advancements, difficulties arise in the ability of researchers to bridge the gaps that exist between the scientist and non-scientist (businessman, manager, pilot, etc.) The need to bridge the gap between these stakeholders intensifies as global awareness of wildlife incidents increases.

The Embry-Riddle Aeronautical University-Prescott International Center for Aviation and Wildlife Risk Mitigation stimulates and supports aviation and wildlife research both within and among the various academic departments and research organizations of the University. The broad research focus includes wildlife risk mitigation, management techniques, legal issues, safety analysis, risk assessment, flight modification and action plans, engineering design, and communication with a view to the optimum present and future dissemination of information.

International Civil Aviation Organization (ICAO) has indicated that safety is a three legged stool consisting of technology, training and regulation.  Missing from the aviation wildlife research outcomes are the last two legs.  Aviation wildlife research that leads to increased safety must incorporate all three components in reaching out to stakeholders. Understanding each of the components and the relationship between them requires explicit communication between the aviation community, regulators, and the public.

The ICAWRM will disseminate information and provide a forum, so that each user/concerned entity can be involved in the development of plans and/or regulations, similar to those created within the aviations community for wind shear, volcanic ash, and ground deicing. 

Advisory Council

The ICAWRM is governed by an Advisory Council and Coordinating Board. The Advisory Council consists of representatives of the Federal Aviation Administration, United State Department of Agriculture-Wildlife Services, Department of Defense-Air Force and Navy, Bird Strike International committee, general aviation, corporate aviation, major airlines, industry, and representatives from major airports. Coordinating Board

The Coordinating Board is comprised of academic members from the ERAU campuses and serves as the governing body of the ICAWRM. The Council participates with the Coordinating Board members in reviewing research proposals, serving on committees and discussing business matters at the annual meeting. The final decision on funding research proposals and matters which require a formal vote is reserved for the Coordinating Board.
