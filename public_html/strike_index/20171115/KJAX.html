Wildlife Strike Summary and Risk Analysis Report

Airport: JACKSONVILLE INTL - (KJAX), 2011-2015

This report provides a 5-part summary of wildlife strikes with civil aircraft for KJAX, 2011-2015.


In Part 1, the total number of strikes at ≤1500 and >1500 feet AGL and the number of those strikes causing an adverse effect are presented for 2013 and for 2011-2015. In Part 2, the adverse effect strike rate for strikes at ≤1500 feet AGL for KJAX is compared to mean values for all airports in KJAX's FAA Region and in the USA that averaged over 50,000 aircraft movements per year, 2011-2015. Part 3 provides a simple wildlife species risk analysis for adverse effect strikes occurring at ≤1500 feet AGL to assist in setting species-specific risk management priorities at KJAX. Part 4 provides a list of all adverse effect strikes occurring at >1500 feet AGL. Part 5 provides a comparison of the adverse effect strike rate for strikes at >1500 feet AGL at KJAX with regional and national means as was done in Part 2 for adverse effect strikes at ≤1500 feet AGL.


As described above, the strike data are separated into 2 components: 1) strikes occurring at ≤1500 feet AGL and 2) strikes occurring during approach or departure at >1500 feet AGL. Aircraft on approach at a 3-degree glide slope are within 5 miles of the airport at 1500 feet. Aircraft on departure are generally much closer to the airport at 1500 feet. Strikes occurring at ≤1500 feet AGL are generally within the 5-mile purview of an airport's Wildlife Hazard Management Plan (WHMP, see FAA AC 150/5200-33B regarding separation distances from an airport's Air Operations Area and hazardous wildlife attractants). Strikes occurring at >1500 feet AGL may be beyond the range of influence of the airport's traditional WHMP but are of interest to air carriers, Air Traffic Control, and community planners in developing strategies to mitigate the risk of these off-airport strikes on approach and departure.


It is important to note that for Parts 2 and 5, comparisons of KJAX with regional and national means are only made using adverse effect strike rates and not total strike rates. Total reported strikes for an airport is not a valid metric for measuring the effectiveness of the airport's WHMP because hazard levels of wildlife species struck vary among airports and some airports are more diligent in reporting all strikes involving even the smallest of birds. In contrast, adverse effect strikes are potential precursors to catastrophic events and constitute a valid metric for measuring risk.

Strike data are taken from reports entered into the FAA National Wildlife Strike Database (see http://wildlife.faa.gov for the latest annual report on national wildlife strike statistics). Aircraft movement data include itinerate air carrier, commuter, and air taxi movements and itinerate and local (i.e., aircraft takes off and lands at same airport) General Aviation movements ( http://aspm.faa.gov/apowtaf/). Military aircraft movements are excluded because military strike data are not included in these analyses.

aThis ranking of wildlife species as to their risk to aviation safety at KJAX is based on the species reported as causing adverse effect to aircraft at and in the vicinity of KJAX during the past 5 years. However, this ranking is not meant to imply that all other wildlife species observed on or near KJAX can be ignored. Some of these other species, because of their size or flocking behavior, may pose a risk that has not been identified (e.g., the species has caused an adverse effect strike that was not reported, the species has caused an adverse effect strike but was identified as "unknown bird"), or the species has not manifested itself yet in an adverse effect strike.
